Mobile

Management At Your Fingertips

This pattern gallery is for product managers, designers, and developers who are creating Elementum mobile applications. As we are defining and refining our mobile strategy, it can be a challenge to find examples of design best practices, especially for multiple operating systems. Whether you have been tasked with designing a simple iPhone app, Android app, or a mobile operating system to be named later, these patterns will provide solutions to common challenges.

The patterns below represent a white label collection of features that are typical across all of Elementum's current apps: Transport, Exposure, and Perspective as well as any future mobile apps.

 

Log in

Open space for individual app branding. Remember me check box for frictionless return visits. Password recovery link. Waitlist choreography for new releases.


 

Intro screens

A simple five-step overview of the app capabilities. Designed with paralax backgrounds and feature highlights that pop-up with personality.


 

Splash

Our splash screen is designed to reinforce our brand while loading the latest data for the returning user who is already logged in. This screen bypasses the login step completely and should appear for only a few seconds.


 

Header / Tabbed navigation

A reflection of our desktop pattern experience. Solid black masthead with title and left-aligned app logo to open the left navigation (see below). A charcoal sub-nav bar, with tabbed selections, give the user easy navigation. Side scrolling for 4 or more navigation tabs are permited in our patterns.


 

Map space

Viewable in portrait and landscape orientations. Portrait keeps some menu choices steadfast to the header. Landscape removes all menu choices except the ability to go back to the previous screen, assuming the user only wants to view/interact with the map.


 

Left navigation

Elementum's home base. Jump to any main page within the app using the sliding left nav. May also include extra information about our platform, updates, and news.


 

Cards

Elementum's deck of data.
