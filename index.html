Our Design Principles

UX follows these four guiding principles when it comes to design

Keep it Simple & Clean

"Good design is obvious. Great design is transparent.” - Joe Sparano

Content can pile on very quickly, but if everything is important – than nothing is important. Be thoughtful and selective when reducing the clutter. Build your designs to be unobtrusive, digestible and intuitive as we assist our users. Don’t add fluff for fancy, but rather make it surprisingly easy.

Know Thy User

"You must read the book before designing the cover.” - Unknown

In this industry, we can’t rely on peoples’ business cards to tell you what they really do. Responsibilities and goals tend overlap job titles. It is vital that we understand our user’s needs and avoid designing on assumptions.

Empower our Users

"Design needs to be plugged into human behavior. Design dissolves in behavior.” - Naoto Fukasawa

If we aren't giving our users content that is insightful than we’re in the wrong business. That said, quality data isn’t enough. Guide them to the data they need to take action and give them a direct path to do so.

Give it Personality

"If we want users to like our software we should design it to behave like a likeable person: respectful, generous and helpful." - Alan Cooper

This ain’t your grandparents' ERP. Today users should expect a delightful experience from their enterprise software. We want to deliver a welcoming environment that invokes elegance and delight.
